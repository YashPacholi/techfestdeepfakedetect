IF YOU ARE A VICTIM — DO THIS IMMEDIATELY
01
STOP ALL TRANSACTIONS
Immediately stop any ongoing payment or transfer. Contact your bank to freeze the account if money was stolen. Call bank's 24/7 helpline.
02
CALL 1930 IMMEDIATELY
The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering money. Cyber crime helpline can freeze the fraudster's account within hours of reporting.
03
TAKE SCREENSHOTS OF EVERYTHING
Screenshot all messages, transaction IDs, phone numbers, email addresses, and any other evidence. Do NOT delete any messages.
04
FILE COMPLAINT ON cybercrime.gov.in
Go to cybercrime.gov.in → Report Cyber Crime → Fill in details. You will get a complaint number to track your case.
05
CHANGE ALL PASSWORDS
Immediately change passwords for email, banking apps, UPI, and social media. Enable 2-factor authentication everywhere.
06
VISIT LOCAL CYBER POLICE STATION
Carry all evidence, transaction receipts, and screenshots. File a physical FIR at your nearest cyber crime police station.
// COMMON CYBER SCAMS IN INDIA
🔲
UPI QR CODE FRAUD
Fake QR codes that debit money instead of crediting. Never scan QR codes from unknown sources. Always verify merchant name.
📱
KYC UPDATE SCAM
Fake calls claiming your SIM/bank/Paytm KYC needs update. Banks never ask for OTP or PIN over phone.
🎭
DEEPFAKE VIDEO SCAM
AI-generated videos of family members asking for emergency money. Always call back on known number to verify.
💼
FAKE JOB OFFER
Offers requiring registration fees or training fees. Legitimate companies never ask for money to hire you.
🏦
LOAN APP FRAUD
Instant loan apps that steal contacts and photos then blackmail users. Only use RBI-registered lending apps.
🎰
LOTTERY / PRIZE FRAUD
Messages claiming you won a prize — requires fees to claim. No legitimate lottery asks for money upfront.
